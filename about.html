Start to My Life

For the first seven years of my life, I grew up in Taipei, Taiwan, surrounded by everything I could want. But despite the comfort, it was a routine, almost mechanical childhood: wake up, go to school, spend long hours in tutoring until 9:00 PM, return home, eat dinner, sleep, repeat. It was normal, even expected—but somewhere in that rhythm, I began to wonder what life could look like outside the structure.

Moving to America

Without understanding the weight of being a first-generation immigrant, I walked into my first day of school in America with little more than a basic grasp of English and a hopeful attitude. I went from failing my English classes to devouring 20 books a week from the local library—desperately trying to catch up, to belong, to understand.

What I Have Learned

After my sophomore year, I entered the summer unsure of where I was headed, but with a quiet determination to figure it out. Surrounded by new experiences and meaningful conversations, something inside me shifted. My relationship with learning, purpose, and ambition began to take a different shape—less about checking boxes and more about building something that mattered to me.

My Future

Penn represents more than just a college—it’s a launching point for the person I want to become. With my sights set on a career in medicine, I look forward to exploring every opportunity, both in and beyond the classroom, that will help me grow, contribute, and define my path.
